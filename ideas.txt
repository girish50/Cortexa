## Global Food Traceability System (Hackathon Project)

Imagine when you buy **rice, milk, vegetables, or fruits**, you usually **don’t know where it came from**, how it was stored, or whether chemicals were used.
This project solves that problem.

---

## 1️⃣ The Real-World Problem

Today the food supply chain is **not transparent**.

A simple example of a tomato’s journey:

Farmer → Local trader → Distributor → Warehouse → Retail store → Consumer

At each stage:

* Quality may change
* Chemicals may be added
* Food may be stored improperly
* Expiry dates may be modified

Consumers **cannot verify anything**.

This causes problems like:

* Food adulteration
* Fake organic labeling
* Unsafe storage
* Expired food resold
* Farmers not getting recognition

---

## 2️⃣ The Main Idea

Create a **platform that tracks food from farm to consumer**.

Every step of the food journey is **recorded and verified**.

When a consumer scans a **QR code**, they can see:

* Which farm produced the food
* When it was harvested
* Transport route
* Storage temperature
* Retail location

So the food becomes **completely traceable**.

---

## 3️⃣ Simple Example

Imagine you buy a **packet of rice**.

You scan the QR code.

The app shows:

Farm Information

* Farmer: Ramesh
* Location: Andhra Pradesh
* Harvest Date: 12 Jan 2026

Transport

* Truck ID: TRK102
* Transport Date: 14 Jan

Warehouse

* Temperature: 18°C
* Storage Duration: 5 days

Retail

* Store: Reliance Fresh
* Arrived: 20 Jan

Now you **know the full journey of the food**.

---

## 4️⃣ Why Blockchain is Used

Normally companies can **edit database records**.

Blockchain prevents this.

Blockchain properties:

* Data cannot be changed
* Every transaction is recorded
* Transparent system
* No cheating

Example:

If someone tries to change

Harvest date:
12 Jan → 20 Jan

Blockchain **will not allow modification**.

It stores a **permanent record**.

---
